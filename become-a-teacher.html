Become a Teacher

eArabic is always keen to communicate with qualified teachers who share the same vision and are seeking opportunities to join us. This page provides information about the job requirements and the working conditions.

The role:

Arabic language teacher for non-native speakers, for children between the ages of six and fourteen.

Personal requirements:

• The applicant must be a resident of Egypt.
• The applicant must have a university degree specialized in Arabic language.
• The applicant must have an experience of three years or more in teaching Arabic.
• Good communication in English.
• Advanced computer skills.
• Previous experience teaching online is preferred.

Working conditions:

• The work is from home during evenings.
• Thirty (30) working hours per week, through one of the following two forms (optional):

  - 6 days x 5 hours
  - 5 days x 6 hours

• The work covers paid leave as follows:

- Week-ends (To be coordinated between teachers).
- Three weeks of annual leave.
- Sick leave satisfying the company's rules and regulations.

Frequently Asked Questions
Can I join you on a part-time basis?
We are sorry, you cannot. Working hours are limited to one of the two forms mentioned above.
How can I ensure my CV was successfully submitted?
Upon submitting your CV, a message should be displayed to confirm the submission was successful. Please note that there will be no email to follow.
Will I be notified if my application was not successful?
Due to the high volume of applications, only successful candidates will be contacted for the next stage.
When should I expect a response if I pass this stage?
The hiring process is an ongoing process, so it is difficult to advise of a particular timeline as of when to notify successful candidates.
What’s next if I passed this stage?
If you passed this stage, you will be contacted for an interview, and will be requested to submit a recorded lesson to further evaluate your skills and capabilities.
Can I inquire about the salary range?
If you pass this stage, you will be contacted to discuss the financial aspect.
Should I send my CV in Arabic or in English?
CVs are accepted in both languages; Arabic and English.
Can I contact recruitment directly?
Communication relevant to recruitment is limited to what is offered on this page.
